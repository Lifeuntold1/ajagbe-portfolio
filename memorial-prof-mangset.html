Fastened to the Rock which cannot move,
Grounded firm and deep in the Saviour’s love.

When the waters cold chill our latest breath;
On the rising tide it can never fail,
While our hopes abide within the veil.
Refrain We have an anchor that keeps the soul

Fastened to the Rock which cannot move,

Hymn 2

In Christ Alone

Words & Music: Keith Getty & Stuart Townend (2001)

Piano Instrumental
1 In Christ alone my hope is found,
He is my light, my strength, my song;
This Cornerstone, this solid Ground,
Firm through the fiercest drought and storm.
What heights of love, what depths of peace,
When fears are stilled, when strivings cease!
My Comforter, my All in All,
Here in the love of Christ I stand.
2 In Christ alone! — who took on flesh,
Fullness of God in helpless babe.
This gift of love and righteousness,
Scorned by the ones He came to save.
Till on that cross as Jesus died,
The wrath of God was satisfied —
For every sin on Him was laid;
Here in the death of Christ I live.
3 There in the ground His body lay,
Light of the world by darkness slain:
Then bursting forth in glorious Day
Up from the grave He rose again!
And as He stands in victory
Sin’s curse has lost its grip on me,
For I am His and He is mine —
Bought with the precious blood of Christ.
4 No guilt in life, no fear in death,
This is the power of Christ in me;
From life’s first cry to final breath,
Jesus commands my destiny.
No power of hell, no scheme of man,
Can ever pluck me from His hand;
Till He returns or calls me home,
Here in the power of Christ I’ll stand!

Rock of Ages, cleft for me,

A Tribute of Gratitude

Honouring a Visionary Leader

Professor Emmanuel Williams Mangset led the Department of Physics with quiet strength and clarity. He did not simply teach physics; he taught us to see the order, beauty, and precision of the universe. His lectures challenged us, his mentorship guided us, and his dedication set a standard we carry forward.

We express our deepest gratitude to the university administration, the academic staff, the visiting delegates, and the student body who stood with us. Your presence and participation in the solidarity honour walk brought comfort and strength to his family and the entire department.

Social Planning Committee

400 Level, Department of Physics

University of Jos
